~1 hr east, iconic waterfall, easy short walk for kids

Pros / cons
+ Makes full use of 4-day window — real trip feel
+ Best August weather of any destination
+ Enchanted Forest + Oaks Park = strong kid anchors
+ Adult experience is genuinely excellent
− ~$2,800–3,500+ before food and activities
− No beach/water fallback for kids
− Flight logistics with two young kids

Day trip options
Great America — Planet Snoopy
15 min away. Closing 2027 — may be last chance. Florence rides ~10 attractions solo or with adult
Gilroy Gardens
45 min south. Small, quiet, built for young kids. Botanical setting, low crowds
Santa Cruz Boardwalk + Beach
1.5 hrs. Beach + classic rides right on the sand. Best water option on this list
Happy Hollow (San Jose)
Very close. Zoo + amusement park designed for young children
Children's Fairyland (Oakland)
1.5 hrs. Storybook-themed park for under-6s. Florence's exact demographic
Sample 4-day itinerary
Thu Aug 6
Great America Planet Snoopy — weekday = less busy, last years of operation
Fri Aug 7
Gilroy Gardens or Happy Hollow — low-key, Florence-paced
Sat Aug 8
Santa Cruz Beach + Boardwalk — beach day with rides
Sun Aug 9
Children's Fairyland or flex/rest day
Pros / cons
+ Saves ~$2,000–3,000 vs. Portland
+ Great America closing soon — timely visit
+ No packing, no airport, no logistics stress
+ Santa Cruz = beach water win for kids
+ Each day independently scoped — easy to cancel/change
− Doesn't feel like a "real trip" — no getaway quality
− No hotel pool or change of scenery
− Adult experience is thinner
The key question: Is the "real trip" feeling worth ~$2,000–3,000 extra? Portland is the better adult trip; day trips are the better kid-per-dollar value. The Great America closing argument is real — Planet Snoopy in 2027 may be gone.
