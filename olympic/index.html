🍃 Hoh Rainforest — Hall of Mosses + Spruce Trail ⭐
This is the crown jewel of the whole trip. Two beautiful flat walks through ancient forest, back to back — together just under 2 miles, totally flat, stroller-friendly. About 80 minutes with the little ones.
Hall of Mosses — enormous trees draped head-to-toe in thick green moss. Completely silent and magical, like walking into a fairy tale.
Spruce Nature Trail — follows the Hoh River through 200-foot spruce trees. Completely different, equally stunning.
2 miles · completely flat⭐ Most magical spot of the trip

Day 6 · Monday, August 24
🏙️ A Full Day to Explore Seattle!
No rushing, no schedule. A completely free day in a wonderful city.
8:30 am
🐟 Pike Place Market
Seattle's famous public market — go early before the crowds! Watch the fishmongers throw whole salmon over the counter (it's a real show!). Get fresh hot mini donuts from Daily Dozen Doughnut Co. Let the kids find the famous gum wall tucked into Post Alley below. Pick up smoked salmon to bring home.
~1 hour
9:30 am
🐙 Seattle Aquarium ⭐ Kid Favorite!
Just a 5-minute walk from Pike Place, right on the waterfront. Touch tanks where the children can pet sea urchins and sea anemones (they'll remember the tide pools from Sunday!), harbor seals, playful river otters, and a giant Pacific octopus. Perfectly sized for young children — 1.5 hours and they'll be satisfied without overload.
⭐ The kids will love this · ~1.5 hrs
11:00 am
🍟 Lunch at Ivar's Fish Bar — Pier 54
A classic Seattle experience. Fish and chips eaten outside on the pier while watching the big ferries come and go. The seagulls are very bold and will absolutely try to steal your fries — the children will find this hilarious. Or try clam chowder in a sourdough bread bowl from Pike Place Chowder just a block away!
🍽️ Lunch 11:00 am · ~45 min
11:45 am

12:00 pm
🗼 Space Needle + Chihuly Glass Garden ⭐
Buy a combo ticket for both — they're right next to each other! The Space Needle observation deck has 360° views: the Olympic Mountains (the very ridge hiked on Friday!), Mount Rainier, Elliott Bay, and the whole city.
Chihuly Garden and Glass is a greenhouse filled with enormous hand-blown glass sculptures in extraordinary colors — like walking into a stained glass wonderland. Absolutely jaw-dropping for children and adults alike.
And the International Fountain in the plaza is a free splash zone on warm days — the toddler will be in absolute heaven!
⭐ Don't skip this · 2.5–3 hours
5:00 pm
🍷 Last Night Dinner
Capitol Hill or South Lake Union for something lively and local. Or stay by the waterfront — Elliott Bay at sunset with the Olympics silhouetted behind it is unforgettable. The sun sets after 8:30pm in August — plenty of time for a long, lovely dinner.
