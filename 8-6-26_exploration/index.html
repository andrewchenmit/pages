Good mornings, monsoon afternoons
August is monsoon season. Mornings are stunning — sunny, dry, 75°F. Afternoon thunderstorms roll in around 2–4pm most days and pass quickly. Plan outdoors before noon.
Meow Wolf is the killer app. Base in Santa Fe (1 hr from ABQ airport), lead with Meow Wolf as the anchor, add Bandelier for scrambling kids. The monsoon rhythm actually maps perfectly onto a toddler schedule — morning out, afternoon shelter. Most culturally interesting destination on this list.
  • 🌀

  • 🪨

  • 🎈
    Balloon Fiesta Grounds (Gondola)Even off-festival season, the tram and grounds are worth a visit. Hot air balloon rides for families available year-round
  • 🏛
    New Mexico Children's MuseumHands-on exhibits in Albuquerque — good rainy-afternoon backup during a monsoon
  • 🌿
    Santa Fe Plaza & Canyon RoadFlat, walkable, colorful. Kids love the fountain at the Plaza and street performers
  • 🍽
    Santa Fe Restaurant SceneThe Shed, Cafe Pasqual's, Joseph's — outstanding New Mexican cuisine. Red or green chile is a revelation
  • 🎨
    Georgia O'Keeffe MuseumWorld-class collection in a beautiful space. Kids can tag along for an hour without protest
  • 🌄
    Taos Day Trip1.5 hrs north — Taos Pueblo (UNESCO site), dramatic gorge bridge, charming art town. A full rewarding day
  • 🧘
    Ten Thousand WavesJapanese mountain spa outside Santa Fe. Private family soaking pools available — kids welcome
  • 🚃
    Sandia Peak TramwayWorld's longest aerial tram from ABQ up to 10,378 ft. Views are stunning; kids love the ride

Excellent — August is Portland's golden window
The one month Portland reliably behaves. Sunny, low humidity, rarely above 85°F. Almost no rain. Don't visit any other month.
Best for adults who want a real trip. Outstanding food scene, beautiful city, Oregon Coast 90 min away. Kids are genuinely well-served too — great children's museum, parks, beaches. Feels like a grown-up trip that works for the whole family.

Hot but manageable — resort pools save the day
August is Cabo's hottest and most humid month, and hurricane season is technically active. That said, actual storms are rare and resort zones with pools make the heat a non-issue. Medano Beach has calm, swimmable water.
Best resort experience closest to Hawaii vibes. The all-inclusive or luxury resort model makes Cabo uniquely low-planning — everything is on-site. Medano Beach is one of the only swimmable beaches in the area; the Pacific side is too rough. Great adult destination that happens to work for kids too.
  • 🏖

  • 🏊

  • 🐬

  • 🎣
    Deep Sea FishingCabo is world-famous for marlin and dorado. Half-day charter while kids stay at the resort pool
  • 🍹
    Sunset CruiseCatamaran sunset sail with open bar past El Arco. One of the best evenings you can have here. Kids welcome
  • 🏜

    Dinner at Edith's or MantaCabo has legitimately great restaurants. Manta at Cape Hotel is a standout. Reserve ahead

Warm and lush — afternoon rain is part of it
August is PV's green season — hot and humid, with afternoon showers most days. Rain is usually brief and dramatic, not all-day. Mornings are reliably gorgeous. The jungle is incredibly lush.
More soul than Cabo, more adventure than a resort bubble. PV has a real town — cobblestone Malecón, great local food, jungle canopy tours. The beach zone (Hotel Zone) is family-friendly with calm bay water. Feels like Mexico, not just a beach compound.

Excellent — ocean breezes keep it comfortable
Pacific coast in August is warm but not brutal. Ocean breezes on deck make it feel cooler than land. Ensenada itself is mid-70s. No rain. Ideal cruise weather.
Two ships, same dates — you're choosing between them. Southwest flies SJC→LGB direct (~1.5 hrs, ~$195 RT/pp). Carnival Firenze departs Long Beach — terminal is just 15 min from LGB airport, making this one of the smoothest door-to-ship logistics on the list. Royal Caribbean Quantum departs San Pedro (~30 min Uber from LGB). RC has more kid amenities and a flashier ship; Carnival is more budget-friendly with a beautiful Italian-designed interior. Both are floating resorts with kids' clubs, pools, and food included.
  • 🏊
    Pool Deck & WaterslidesBoth ships have multiple pools and waterslides. RC Quantum has a FlowRider surf simulator and waterpark. Toddlers love the splash zones — this alone fills a sea day
  • 🎨
    Kids' Club (Camp Ocean / Adventure Ocean)RC and Carnival both run supervised kids' clubs for ages 2+. Drop Florence off for a few hours — genuinely fun for her, genuinely relaxing for you
  • 🌮
    Ensenada Port DayOne full day in Ensenada. La Bufadora blowhole is a 30-min drive and kids love the dramatic spray. Avenida López Mateos has great street tacos and churros
  • 🎭
    Live Shows & EntertainmentBoth ships have Broadway-style productions and family shows each evening. Kids this age are riveted by the spectacle
  • 🍦
    Unlimited Ice Cream & FoodThe buffet never closes. Kids' food stations, pizza, ice cream machines, burgers — all included. A toddler's dream scenario
  • 🍽
    Specialty DiningRC has Wonderland (avant-garde tasting menu) and Izumi. Carnival has Fahrenheit 555 Steakhouse and Bonsai Teppanyaki. Book a night out while kids are in club
  • 🌅
    Sea Day Deck LoungingOne full day at sea each way. Nothing to plan. Drink package, deck chair, ocean horizon. The antidote to overthinking vacations
  • 🎰
    Casino & NightlifeBoth ships have casinos, bars, and live music venues active after kids' bedtime. Easy to enjoy once Florence is down
  • 🧖
    Spa (Cloud 9 / RC Spa)Full spa on both ships. Carnival's Cloud 9 Spa is well-regarded. Book sea-day appointments — they fill up fast
  • 🍷
    Ensenada Wine CountryValle de Guadalupe is Baja's famous wine region, 30 min from the port. Excellent for an afternoon tour while grandparents or the ship's club watches kids

Hot but manageable — park has shade and A/C
Anaheim in August is sunny and dry, around 88–92°F. No rain. Hot for standing in lines but the park has plenty of shade, indoor rides, and misting stations. Early arrival essential.
The pure kid maximalist pick. Florence is at the exact perfect age for Disneyland — old enough to understand the magic, young enough to believe it completely. No other destination on this list generates the same level of kid joy per hour. The adult experience is more "along for the ride" but genuinely fun if you lean in. Logistics are extremely easy.
  • 🏰
    Fantasyland — It's a Small World, Dumbo, CarouselThe toddler zone. All rides are appropriate for Florence's age, low wait times in early morning. This is her entire world right now
  • 🚂
    Disneyland RailroadCircles the whole park. Great rest ride for little legs — sit down, see everything, no effort required
  • 🌟
    Character Meet & GreetsMickey, Minnie, princesses — book Lightning Lane or get there at rope drop. The photos are worth the trip alone
  • 🎠
    Mickey's ToontownRedesigned in 2023 specifically for toddlers — splash zone, Mickey's house, Chip & Dale Treehouse. Peak Florence territory
  • 🏄
    California Adventure — Radiator SpringsCars Land is stunning. Mater's Junkyard Jamboree is perfect for her age; Luigi's Rollickin' Roadsters too
  • 🎢
    Indiana Jones & Matterhorn (early morning)Hit these first at rope drop before lines build. Classic adult-worthy rides that still hold up
  • 🍽
    Blue Bayou RestaurantInside Pirates of the Caribbean — atmospheric, genuinely good food, a rare grown-up dining moment inside the park. Reserve ahead
  • 🌆
    Galaxy's Edge at DuskStar Wars land is most magical at night. Millennium Falcon ride is excellent. Kids and adults equally wowed
  • 🍸
    Carthay Circle Lounge (DCA)The only real bar in the parks. Excellent cocktails, nice respite while kids nap in stroller outside
  • 🎆
    Fantasmic! Nighttime ShowSpectacular water, fire, and projection show on the Rivers of America. Both parks have evening spectaculars — worth staying late once

Best weather on this list — genuinely perfect
August is Victoria's warmest and sunniest month. Highs around 68–72°F, almost zero rain, low humidity. The most reliably comfortable weather of any destination here. Light jacket in the evenings.
⚠️ No direct SJC flight — requires connection or ferry via Seattle. Best option: fly SJC→SEA (2 hrs) + Kenmore Air floatplane to Victoria harbor (35 min) — actually magical. Or fly to Vancouver and ferry. Worth flagging since this was your constraint, but the destination itself is exceptional for families.
  • 🐋
    Whale WatchingOrcas, humpbacks, and minkes frequent the Salish Sea in August. Best whale watching in North America. Kids remember it forever
  • 🌸
    Butchart GardensWorld-famous sunken garden — 55 acres of flowers. Kids go for the Saturday night fireworks display; daytime is equally stunning
  • 🦦
    Victoria Bug Zoo & Miniature WorldTwo quirky attractions downtown kids love — giant insects to hold and an absurdly detailed miniature world
  • 🚲
    Inner Harbour Bike RideFlat, scenic waterfront path. Rent a quadricycle for the family — kids ride along in style
  • 🏖
    Willows Beach / Gyro ParkCalm sheltered beach with playground. Not tropical but beautiful — clean, uncrowded, perfect August afternoon
  • 🍺
    Victoria Craft Beer SceneOne of Canada's best brewery cities — Driftwood, Phillips, Hoyne. Many patios welcome kids in the afternoon
  • 🫖
    Afternoon Tea at the EmpressThe Fairmont Empress hotel afternoon tea is iconic. Dress up the kids — they have a children's menu version
  • 🌊
    Kayaking the Gulf IslandsHalf-day guided sea kayak tour from the harbor. Calm, beautiful, wildlife everywhere. Kids 5+ can join some tours
  • 🍽
    Dinner on Government StExcellent seafood, Pacific Northwest cuisine. Brasserie L'École and Agrius are standouts
  • 🏛
    Royal BC MuseumWorld-class natural history and First Nations collections. Kids love the life-size woolly mammoth
